\noindent{\bf 2.}
\begin{align*}
    \frac{\hat k-1}{2} &= \frac1n\sum_{i=1}^nY_i \\
    \hat k &= 1 + \frac2n\sum_{i=1}^nY_i
\end{align*}

\medskip
\noindent{\bf 3.}
\begin{align*}
    \hat k &= 1 + \frac2n\sum_{i=1}^nY_i \\
           &= 1 + \frac23(3 + 4 + 5) \\
           &= 9.
\end{align*}

\medskip
\noindent{\bf 4.}
\begin{align*}
    \hat k &= 1 + \frac2n\sum_{i=1}^nY_i \\
           &= 1 + \frac23(3 + 4 + 6) \\
           &= 9 + \frac23.
\end{align*}

\medskip
\noindent{\bf 5.}
\begin{align*}
    \hat k &= 1 + \frac2n\sum_{i=1}^nY_i \\
           &= 1 + \frac23(3 + 4 + 26) \\
           &= 23.
\end{align*}

\medskip
\noindent{\bf 6.} $\hat k$ is a pretty weird guess. In question 3 it seems pretty good, but in question 4 it's not a possibility at all. Question 5 it also seems like a weak guess, but I'm not sure how to make a better guess than that.
